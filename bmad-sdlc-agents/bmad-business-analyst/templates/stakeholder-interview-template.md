## Stakeholder Context

**Understanding who this person is and their role in the initiative.**

### Role Description

[2-3 sentences describing what this person does day-to-day, their responsibilities, and how they interact with the system/process being analyzed.]

Example: "Sarah is the VP of Sales for the North America region. She manages a team of 15 sales reps and is responsible for $10M annual revenue. She uses our CRM daily to track deals, forecast pipeline, and review sales metrics."

### Decision-Making Authority

Select the RACI level for this stakeholder in the initiative:

- **Accountable:** Final decision-maker; owns success/failure of initiative
- **Responsible:** Does the work; implements decisions
- **Consulted:** Provides input; asked for opinion before decision
- **Informed:** Kept in the loop; notified of decisions/progress

[In this case: **Select One**]

### Business Criticality to Initiative

- **Critical:** Initiative directly impacts this person's job; their buy-in essential
- **High:** Initiative impacts their team; their needs must be addressed
- **Medium:** Initiative somewhat relevant; nice-to-have input
- **Low:** Initiative peripheral to their role; advisory input only

## Interview Objectives

**What you want to learn from this stakeholder. 2-3 clear goals.**

- [ ] Understand their current process/workflow and pain points
- [ ] Identify success metrics and priorities for improvement
- [ ] Discover hidden dependencies or constraints
- [ ] Assess appetite for change and potential resistance

## Opening Questions

**5 open-ended questions to understand current state and context. Avoid yes/no questions.**

### Q1: Role & Responsibilities
"Can you walk me through your typical day? What are the main tasks you focus on, and what tools do you use?"

**Purpose:** Understand daily context, existing tools, process flow.

---

### Q2: Current Pain Points
"What are your biggest frustrations with the current system or process? Where do you spend the most time and get the least value?"

**Purpose:** Identify problems from their perspective; don't assume what the problem is.

---

### Q3: Success Metrics
"How do you measure success in your role? What KPIs or metrics matter most to you and your team?"

**Purpose:** Learn how they define "good" and "better"; metrics they care about.

---

### Q4: Constraints & Limitations
"What constraints or limitations do you face? Things like budget, time, technical skills, regulations, or organizational policies that impact what you can do?"

**Purpose:** Identify boundaries; understand what's not negotiable.

---

### Q5: Vision & Ideal State
"If there were no constraints, what would your ideal process/system look like? What would change?"

**Purpose:** Understand their blue-sky vision; separates "wish list" from "needs."

---

## Deep Dive Questions

**10 detailed questions exploring specific areas relevant to the initiative.**

### Process Deep Dive

**Q6: Current Workflow Details**
"Walk me through the process of [specific workflow]. How many steps are involved? Who else is involved? Where do bottlenecks happen?"

**Purpose:** Detailed understanding of step-by-step process; identify inefficiencies.

---

**Q7: Decision Points**
"In this process, where do decisions get made? Who makes them? How long does a decision typically take?"

**Purpose:** Identify critical decision gates; understand approval/escalation paths.

---

### Data & Information

**Q8: Data Usage**
"What data or information do you need to do your job effectively? Where does it come from? Is it accurate and timely? How often do you need it?"

**Purpose:** Understand data dependencies; assess information quality.

---

**Q9: Reporting & Visibility**
"What reports or dashboards do you use? What metrics do you track? Are you missing any visibility?"

**Purpose:** Identify existing analytics; gaps in insight.

---

### People & Organization

**Q10: Team Dynamics**
"How does your team coordinate with other departments? What collaboration challenges exist? Who do you depend on, and who depends on you?"

**Purpose:** Understand organizational dependencies; cross-functional issues.

---

**Q11: Skill & Capability**
"What skills does your team have? Are there capabilities you're lacking? Do you have training/support needs?"

**Purpose:** Assess readiness for change; identify training needs.

---

### Priorities & Trade-offs

**Q12: Priorities**
"If you could prioritize 3 things to improve in the next 6 months, what would they be? Why are these most important?"

**Purpose:** Understand their top 3; helps with prioritization.

---

**Q13: Trade-offs**
"Sometimes improvements require trade-offs. For example, more automation might reduce flexibility. Are there trade-offs you'd be willing to make? Any you wouldn't?"

**Purpose:** Understand limits; what they won't give up.

---

**Q14: Success Factors**
"What would need to happen for you to consider this initiative successful? What would you need from us, from leadership, from other teams?"

**Purpose:** Define success from their lens; identify support needed.

---

**Q15: Risks & Concerns**
"Are you concerned about anything related to this initiative? What could go wrong? What would you want to avoid?"

**Purpose:** Identify risks early; understand objections.

---

## Closing Questions

**3 questions to summarize learning and identify follow-ups.**

### Q16: Key Takeaways
"Based on what you've shared, what do you think is the most important thing we should focus on?"

**Purpose:** Confirm understanding; get their priority framing.

---

### Q17: Feedback on Interview
"Have we missed anything important? Are there other people we should talk to?"

**Purpose:** Identify gaps; get referrals to other stakeholders.

---

### Q18: Commitment & Next Steps
"If we move forward with this initiative, how would you like to stay involved? What level of participation works for you?"

**Purpose:** Clarify ongoing involvement; set expectations for collaboration.

## Checklist: Interview Completeness

- [ ] All interview metadata recorded (date, time, duration, attendees)
- [ ] At least 5 opening questions asked and answered
- [ ] Deep dive questions covered 3+ topic areas
- [ ] Closing questions asked; next steps identified
- [ ] Key insights extracted and summarized
- [ ] Requirements documented with priorities
- [ ] Direct quotes captured
- [ ] Follow-up actions assigned
- [ ] Recording/notes archived securely
- [ ] Stakeholder alignment assessed
- [ ] Interview summary shared with stakeholder for validation
- [ ] Insights synthesized into requirements document

## Synthesis Across Interviews

**After conducting multiple interviews, synthesize findings. Complete after >3 interviews.**

### Common Themes

[Patterns emerging across multiple stakeholders. What do most/all of them agree on?]

Example:
- Manual data entry is a universal pain point (5/5 stakeholders mentioned)
- ROI proof needed before buy-in (3/5 stakeholders)
- Timeline concerns around holiday season (2/5)

---

### Conflicting Perspectives

[Areas where stakeholders disagree. Which stakeholder's view is prioritized?]

Example:
- Sales wants more features; Operations wants simplicity
- Resolve: Product owner prioritizes; create phased rollout

---

### Unmet Needs

[Gaps identified; needs not being addressed by current system.]

Example:
- Mobile access (4/5 want; not currently supported)
- Real-time notifications (3/5 want; batch reports today)
- Integration with email (2/5 want)
